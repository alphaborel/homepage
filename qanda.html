Some questions and answers for your consideration.

Q. What is your motivation in moving from Aerospace into Web development?

A. Growth, both personal and professional. Over the years as I've helped others with their computers and watched the birth of the internet, smart phones and other connected technologies, I've witnessed the rise of powerful tools that connect people and help them in their daily lives. I want to have some part in that.

Q. What education have you had?

A. I've taken some community college from GCC with Information Systems classes. These days, I am self taught through sites like Lynda, Treehouse, Udemy and others. Though, I find I can accelerate my learning even more if I work with a team and a mentor. I love to read and participate in educational programs of all types. Learning never stops.

Q. The Honeywell Operating System (HOS) from your former company is similar to Agile using Scrum. In what ways is it similar?

A. It was designed to monitor and communicate work to the team. We had daily stand up meetings and a rotating kiosk with four different categories. Similar to Scrum task board methodology. There was also a Flow Specialist to guide the team. Similar to a Scrum Master.

Q. What Operating System do you prefer?

A. Currently it's Windows. I've installed and used many OSs over the years including OSX, and several distributions of Linux but I keep coming back to Windows. I can feel at home with most operating systems but I find that I am most productive on a windows machine.

Q. What is your web development workflow?

A. I am still developing a workflow. Right now, I jump right into coding with a rough idea of what I want things to look like and fill in the details as I go along. My editor of choice is Brackets by Adobe and I use Photoshop to process graphics for my project. Hand coding, with a few code snippets and my notes to move things along.

Q. Can you pass a drug test?

A. Absolutely.
